## Derivaties and Differentiation

Derivatives can tell us how rapidly a loss function would increase or decrease were we to *increase* or *decrease* each parameter by an infinitesimally small amount. For functions $f : \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$  that map from scalars to scalars, the *derivative* of $f$ at a point $x$ is defined as

$$
f'(x) = \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}
$$
